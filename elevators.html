Forecasting Elevators!

Background

So, I really did not like the elevators at our old building

It seemed like an entriniy between the time that you called an elevator and one showed up

I felt that elevators could be made better, and that waiting around to be called was inefficient

My theroy was that in the mornings, everyone probably wanted to catch an elevator from the first floor on their way to work

And in eveneings, the oppostie would occur. Few people if anybody, would catch an elevator from the first floor, but everyone wanted to go there

So, I configued an old financal forcasting model (ARMA) to work with elevators

I used time of day, and floor where the elevator was called as inputs

With the goal of reducing "floors missed" when a person comes to elevator

Process

Finding actual data about what time and what floor people got on an elevators turned out to be very difficult

Who know that kind of data was not just "out there"

So I ended up making a few sets of semi-random data

I collapsed the the exact times into 10 mintue intervals to reduce nosie (My thought is, this would not have a huge impact, because the largest gains came at the beinging of the day)

Then run an ARMA on that data, then compare it to how a elevator would normally preform

I also wanted to compare it to the theoretical situation where the elevator would go to a random floor between trips becuase that sounded fun

Results

On the left were the results of using the forecasted floor vs. a random floor vs. the perious floor

Its kinda hard to tell, but using the forecasted floor made the elevator on avg about 50% closer to where it needed to be

And that moving randomly between pickups was just about as good as waiting

Theoretical next steps would be to get actual data to work with and try out different models to see if I could imporove even more
